## Background

Much of what we do involves technology. Arthur (2009) defines technology as "the orchestration of phenomena for some purpose" (see [[drons-technology]]). Phenomena includes stuff that is "real or imagined, mental or physical, designed or existing in the natural world" (Dron, 2021, p. 2). Language is a technology that orchestrates the phenomena of sounds and symbols for the purpose of communication. A hammer orchestrates material, gravity, and external force for the purpose of driving nails.

The "gather, weave, and augment" idea arose out of [work in higher education learning and teaching enhancement](https://djon.es/blog/2023/02/09/gathers-weavers-and-augmenters-three-principles-for-dynamic-and-sustainable-delivery-of-quality-learning-and-teaching/) thinking about how we could improve how we orchestrated digital technology to support learning and teaching.

The idea is that an activity being supported with digital technology involves the three key functions of _gathering_ information, _weaving_ disparate information together, and _augmenting_ that information in some way.

!!! note "Contextual customisation as the missing element"

  A key idea from the original work is that the type of gather/weave/augment supported by most digital technology - especially institutionally approved digital technology in organisations - is generic with limited capacity for specific contextual customisation.

  That absence is typically addressed through either significant manual human activity or [[shadow-system|shadow systems]]. See more [[bad_set-casa-gather-weave|relationship between the ideas of BAD/SET, CASA, and Gather/Weave/Augument]].
